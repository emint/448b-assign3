This project attempts to visualize how great people traveled between ancient Greek cities. Nodes represent Greek cities and edges exist if two cities have had mroe than one common person visit them.

To begin, please hover over a node. This will show you its connection as well as view of the world from its perspective. To exclude categories of people, please uncheck the category of person.

The radius of a node on the main graph is logarithmically proportional to number of important people that have visited that city. Edge widths are proportional to number of people shared between two cities.

On the circle-packing graph, the size is proportional to the number of people shared with the selected city.
